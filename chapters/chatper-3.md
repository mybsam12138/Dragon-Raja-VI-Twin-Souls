# Chapter 3

# The Black King's Fragments

The steel door opened with a sound that had not been heard in decades.

A low metallic groan echoed through the darkness.

Dust fell from the ceiling.

Ancient mechanisms slowly awakened.

---

Lu Mingfei stood beside Guderian.

Neither spoke.

---

The passage beyond the door descended deep underground.

Far deeper than any laboratory.

Far deeper than any archive.

---

The air felt strange.

Heavy.

Ancient.

As though the place itself remembered something humanity had forgotten.

---

"How old is this place?" Lu Mingfei asked.

---

Guderian hesitated.

---

"We don't know."

---

"What do you mean you don't know?"

---

"The earliest Cassell records mention the chamber."

---

"And before that?"

---

"There are no records."

---

Lu Mingfei frowned.

---

"No records?"

---

Guderian nodded.

---

"It simply appears."

---

The answer was ridiculous.

Yet somehow, after everything he had experienced, it no longer sounded impossible.

---

The corridor ended at a circular chamber.

Massive stone pillars surrounded the room.

Ancient symbols covered every surface.

Some resembled dragon language.

Others belonged to no known civilization.

---

At the center stood a black pedestal.

---

Upon it rested a single object.

---

A fragment.

---

No larger than a human hand.

Dark as obsidian.

Its surface seemed to absorb light itself.

---

For a moment, nobody moved.

---

Then Lu Mingfei felt it.

---

A heartbeat.

---

Slow.

Heavy.

Ancient.

---

Thump.

---

The sound echoed through his mind.

---

Thump.

---

Again.

---

His vision blurred.

---

The chamber disappeared.

---

And another memory emerged.

---

A sky filled with dragons.

Golden flames.

Countless wings.

The roar of a dying world.

---

Then a voice.

---

Ancient.

Majestic.

Terrifying.

---

"Nothing truly dies."

---

The words shook the heavens.

---

"Memories survive."

---

The vision changed.

---

Empires rose.

Empires fell.

Mountains became oceans.

Cities vanished beneath the sea.

---

Yet something endured.

---

"Bloodlines survive."

---

The voice continued.

---

Generations passed.

Children inherited forgotten gifts.

Ancient instincts remained hidden within human DNA.

---

"Dragon genes survive."

---

The world shattered once more.

---

Then came darkness.

---

An endless sea of darkness.

---

Within it floated countless fragments of consciousness.

Broken thoughts.

Forgotten dreams.

Echoes of beings long dead.

---

And among them—

One presence.

Vast.

Silent.

Waiting.

---

"Ancient consciousness survives."

---

The voice faded.

---

The vision ended.

---

Lu Mingfei stumbled backward.

---

Cold sweat covered his forehead.

---

"What was that?"

---

No one answered.

---

Because Guderian's face had already turned pale.

---

The professor was staring at the fragment.

---

Not with curiosity.

---

With fear.

---

Real fear.

---

"The records were right."

---

"What records?"

---

Guderian swallowed.

---

Then spoke the words that Cassell College had spent more than a century trying to bury.

---

"The Black King never truly died."

---

Silence.

---

The sentence lingered in the air.

Impossible.

Heretical.

Terrifying.

---

For centuries, every hybrid had learned the same history.

---

Nidhogg had fallen.

The Black King was dead.

The age of dragons had ended.

---

That was the foundation upon which Cassell College had built its mission.

---

The foundation upon which the entire hybrid world existed.

---

And now?

---

One fragment threatened to destroy all of it.

---

Because if the Black King still survived—

Even as memories.

Even as bloodlines.

Even as dragon genes.

Even as ancient consciousness—

Then everything Cassell believed could be wrong.

---

Suddenly, a new voice echoed through the chamber.

---

Not ancient.

Not majestic.

---

Familiar.

---

Painfully familiar.

---

A boy's voice.

---

Soft.

Calm.

Amused.

---

"Looks like you're finally getting close, brother."

---

Lu Mingfei froze.

---

For the first time in years.

---

He heard that voice again.

---

Lu Mingze.

---

The darkness behind the fragment rippled slightly.

---

Golden eyes opened.

---

And smiled.
