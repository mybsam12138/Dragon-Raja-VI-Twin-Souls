# Chapter 1

# The Sleeping Memories

The rain fell over Chicago.

Cold.

Endless.

The city lights blurred behind the glass walls of the dormitory.

Lu Mingfei sat alone in the darkness.

His laptop screen glowed faintly.

An unfinished report remained open.

He had been staring at the same page for nearly twenty minutes.

Without reading a single word.

Something felt wrong.

Not dangerous.

Not urgent.

Just wrong.

Like forgetting an important appointment.

Like waking up from a dream and realizing something precious had been left behind.

---

Outside the window, thunder rolled across the sky.

For a brief moment, the room flashed white.

And suddenly—

He saw it.

Not the dormitory.

Not Chicago.

Not even the modern world.

---

A battlefield.

Black clouds.

Burning cities.

Mountains collapsing into oceans.

The sky itself seemed to be dying.

Countless dragons screamed.

Their wings covered the heavens.

Blood rained from above.

---

Lu Mingfei stood in the middle of the destruction.

No.

Someone stood there.

Someone wearing his face.

And yet not him.

Far older.

Far colder.

Far lonelier.

---

A golden throne rose from the flames.

Above it sat a shadow.

A king.

A dragon.

A god.

Or perhaps all three.

---

Then the king opened his eyes.

Golden.

Ancient.

Terrifying.

---

The king looked directly at him.

Not through him.

Not past him.

At him.

As though waiting.

As though recognizing him.

As though he had always known him.

---

Lu Mingfei woke with a violent gasp.

His chair nearly fell backward.

The room was silent.

Only the sound of rain remained.

His heart pounded.

His hands trembled.

---

"What the hell..."

He rubbed his forehead.

Just a dream.

Another strange dream.

Lately they had become more frequent.

More vivid.

More real.

---

The details never remained.

Only feelings.

Fear.

Loneliness.

Loss.

An unbearable sadness.

As if he had forgotten someone.

Someone important.

Someone he should remember.

But couldn't.

---

His phone vibrated.

A message appeared.

Nono.

"Still awake?"

Lu Mingfei stared at the screen.

After several seconds he replied.

"Can't sleep."

Almost immediately another message arrived.

"Nightmares again?"

He paused.

Then typed:

"Yeah."

Three dots appeared.

Disappeared.

Appeared again.

Finally:

"Maybe you should talk to the psychologist."

---

Lu Mingfei laughed.

A short, helpless laugh.

Somehow that sounded ridiculous.

How was a psychologist supposed to explain dragons?

Or gods?

Or a boy with golden eyes who appeared whenever the world was ending?

---

His smile slowly faded.

Because he suddenly realized something.

Something that made the room feel colder.

---

He could no longer remember the last time he had seen Lu Mingze.

---

The realization struck him like lightning.

For years Lu Mingze had appeared everywhere.

During battles.

During crises.

During impossible choices.

Always smiling.

Always watching.

Always calling him:

"Brother."

---

But now?

Nothing.

No voice.

No shadow.

No contracts.

No appearances.

---

As if he had vanished.

---

The rain continued outside.

The city slept.

The world moved on.

Yet somewhere in the darkness, an old memory stirred.

Something ancient.

Something forgotten.

Something waiting.

---

Far beyond Chicago.

Far beyond Cassell College.

Far beyond human history itself.

A pair of golden eyes slowly opened.

And for the first time in thousands of years,

they remembered the name:

Lu Mingfei.
