# Prologue

## The Girl Beneath the Cherry Blossoms

Several years had passed since the end of the war.

The world moved on.

Cassell College still stood.

The dragons were still gone.

And yet, some stories refused to end.

---

Far north of Tokyo, spring arrived quietly.

Cherry blossoms drifted through the air like pale snow.

Beneath a blooming sakura tree stood a young girl.

She watched the petals fall in silence.

She did not know why she always came here.

Only that something deep within her told her to return every year.

As if she were waiting for someone.

Someone she could not remember.

---

At night, the dreams returned.

They always began the same way.

A crowded train station.

The smell of rain.

The distant sound of departing trains.

People passing by without faces.

A ticket clutched tightly in her hand.

And somewhere within the crowd—

A boy.

She never saw his face.

No matter how hard she tried.

He was always walking away.

Always disappearing.

Always just beyond reach.

---

Sometimes there were cherry blossoms.

Sometimes there was snow.

Sometimes there was only silence.

But the feeling never changed.

A loneliness so deep that it hurt.

A sadness that did not belong to her.

A memory she had never lived.

---

Then came the voice.

Soft.

Familiar.

Painfully familiar.

A voice she felt she had known her entire life.

A voice she could never remember after waking.

---

Every dream ended the same way.

The train doors closing.

The station lights fading.

The boy turning away.

And a single question.

"Will you come back?"

---

She always woke before hearing the answer.

Every time.

Without exception.

---

The girl opened her eyes.

Morning sunlight filtered through the window.

Outside, cherry blossoms danced in the wind.

For a long moment she stared at the ceiling.

Then she smiled faintly and shook her head.

Just another dream.

That was all.

Just a dream.

---

But somewhere far away, beyond the reach of memory,

a lonely boy was finally beginning to remember.

And the world was about to change.

Again.
