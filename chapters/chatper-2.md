# Chapter 2

# The Hidden Archive

The next morning, Chicago was covered in fog.

Cassell College emerged from the mist like an old castle forgotten by time.

Ancient stone walls.

Gothic towers.

Silent courtyards.

Everything looked exactly as it always had.

Yet Lu Mingfei could not shake the feeling that something had changed.

---

Professor Guderian noticed immediately.

"You look terrible."

Lu Mingfei yawned.

"I didn't sleep."

"Nightmares again?"

"Maybe."

---

Guderian stared at him for several seconds.

Then slowly lowered his coffee cup.

"You know," he said quietly, "sometimes memories aren't dreams."

---

Lu Mingfei laughed.

"That's a terrifying thing to say before breakfast."

---

But Guderian wasn't smiling.

---

The professor opened a folder.

Inside was a single photograph.

Old.

Black and white.

At least a hundred years old.

---

The photograph showed a group of hybrid students standing before Cassell's main building.

Nothing unusual.

Until Lu Mingfei looked closer.

---

There.

Standing in the back row.

A young man.

Black hair.

Ordinary face.

Familiar eyes.

---

Lu Mingfei froze.

---

"Impossible."

---

The young man looked exactly like him.

---

Not similar.

Not resembling.

Exactly.

---

The same face.

The same expression.

The same eyes.

Even the same slightly awkward posture.

---

A cold sensation crawled up his spine.

---

"When was this taken?"

---

Guderian looked down at the photograph.

"Officially?"

"One hundred and twelve years ago."

---

Silence filled the room.

---

"That's impossible."

---

"Yes."

---

"Someone altered it?"

---

"We checked."

---

"Fake?"

---

"We checked."

---

"Then what the hell am I looking at?"

---

Guderian didn't answer immediately.

Because he didn't know.

---

For the past three months, strange discoveries had appeared throughout Cassell's archives.

Photographs.

Documents.

Mission reports.

Names.

Faces.

Records that should not exist.

---

All connected by one person.

---

Lu Mingfei.

---

Sometimes the name was different.

Sometimes the identity was different.

But the face never changed.

---

A hybrid hunter in Siberia.

A scholar in Berlin.

A soldier during the Second World War.

A dragon researcher in nineteenth-century London.

---

Always the same face.

---

Always appearing before major dragon-related disasters.

---

Always disappearing afterward.

---

As if someone had been moving through history itself.

---

Lu Mingfei felt a headache forming.

---

"This isn't funny."

---

"No one thinks it's funny."

---

The professor handed him another document.

---

Unlike the photograph, this one was handwritten.

The paper had turned yellow with age.

The ink had faded.

---

Yet one sentence remained clearly visible.

---

*"The subject displays signs of memory retention across multiple lifetimes."*

---

Lu Mingfei stared.

---

"What subject?"

---

Guderian's expression darkened.

---

"We don't know."

---

Another line appeared below.

---

*"The soul remains incomplete."*

---

*"The second half has not yet awakened."*

---

The room suddenly felt very cold.

---

Lu Mingfei slowly lowered the paper.

---

For reasons he could not explain, those words frightened him more than dragons.

More than Odin.

More than death.

---

The second half.

---

A strange image flashed through his mind.

Golden eyes.

A smile.

A familiar voice.

---

Brother.

---

The vision disappeared instantly.

---

Lu Mingfei stood up so suddenly that his chair crashed to the floor.

---

"What archive did this come from?"

---

Guderian hesitated.

---

"There is a sealed section beneath the college."

---

"How sealed?"

---

"Only the principal can access it."

---

"Angers?"

---

Guderian nodded.

---

"The records were hidden before Cassell College was officially founded."

---

That made no sense.

---

Cassell College had existed for over a century.

---

How could records be older than the institution itself?

---

Unless...

---

The organization existed long before the school.

---

And somebody had been hiding something for a very long time.

---

Outside, thunder echoed across the sky.

---

Neither man noticed the old surveillance camera mounted in the corner of the room.

---

A small red light blinked once.

---

Far beneath Cassell College.

Beyond restricted laboratories.

Beyond forgotten tunnels.

Beyond classified archives.

---

A massive steel door slowly unlocked.

---

For the first time in decades.

---

Something inside had awakened.
